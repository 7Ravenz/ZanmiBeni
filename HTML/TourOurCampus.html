Arts Center Centre Des Arts

The Arts Center is used for by the children for a variety of activities including painting, arts and crafts, music, storytelling, and special programs such as visiting artists and musicians. It is also used as a gathering place for visitors and meetings and has two guest bedrooms, each with a private bath. Les enfants ont recours au Centre des arts pour diverses activités, notamment la peinture, l'artisanat, la musique, la narration, et des programmes spéciaux tels que des artistes invités et des musiciens. Il est également utilisé comme lieu de rassemblement pour les visiteurs et les réunions et dispose de deux chambres d'amis, chacune avec une salle de bain privée.


LIBRARY BIBLIOTHÈQUE

Located in the center of the campus, this all-purpose space houses, ZB offices, and a large library. Our children recieve tutoring in this building, and enjoy eating popcorn and watching films in the movie corner. Situé au centre du campus, cet espace polyvalent abrite, les bureaux de ZB et une grande bibliothèque. Nos enfants reçoivent et du tutorat dans ce bâtiment. Nous utilisons également cet espace pour les repas lorsque nous accueillons des groupes sur notre campus.


ST. ROSE OF LIMA DOMINICAN CHAPEL St. Rose la Chapelle Dominicaine de Lima

Our chapel is the spiritual center of our campus and is a peaceful and quiet spot for rest and renewal. Church services for the Zanmi Beni community are held on a regular basis and it is a delight to hear the children singing in the choir. The children actively participate in weekly mass by being alter servers, readers, and singers. It is easy to tell it is Sunday as everyone is dressed up in their “Sunday Best”. The beautiful windows, depicting images of Haiti’s patron, Our Lady of Perpetual Help, Blessed Pierre Toussaint, and five Dominican saints including St. Dominic and St. Rose of Lima were designed by Fr. Cristóbal Torres, OP and executed by Deborah Griffin.


THE CHILDRENS PLAYGROUND Notre Histoire

We are fortunate to have large outdoor space on our campus with plenty of room for exercise and play. All of the children enjoy the colorful playground located in the center of the campus.


KITCHEN AND DINING HALL Notre Histoire

The children and staff are provided with nutritious meals that are prepared in our well-equipped kitchen. The children enjoy and share mealtimes together in our dining hall. As they get older, the children have started assuming responsibility for helping with meal preparation and clean up.


THE KAY ROSE DORMITORY Notre Histoire

Centered around a spacious and bright courtyard and common space for recreation and conversation. Our original dormitory now houses the younger boys, the children with disabilities who share either double or triple rooms. It also contains the nursing station. Our nurses and caregivers are there 24 hours a day, and many have been with the children since 2010. This lovely new building houses the bedrooms, closet space and baths for our children who share either double or triple cheerfully decorated rooms.


ROBERT F. SMITH FAMILY DORMATORY Notre Histoire

Thanks to the generosity of American Business man Robert F. Smith and his family, Our newest dormitory was opened in August 2016. This provided the much needed space for teen members of our family, the girls, staff and visitors.


THERAPIES ROOM Notre Histoire

An array of services including physical and occupational therapy are offered in our Therapy Room.The new therapy room is now in the Robert Smith Family dormitory. The space is also used for games, arts and crafts and dance lessons.


BAKERY Notre Histoire

Our bakery is housed in a six hundred square foot dedicated space on Zanmi Beni’s campus. Operated on a professional scale, the bakery is fully equipped with professional baking equipment including: An 80 quart Hobart mixer 2 Blodgett ovens Industrial refrigerators Stainless steel preparation tables Stainless steel cooling racks storage shelves Our baker, Wisly, produces a wonderful wheat bread which feeds our children and guests to our campus. The breadmaking takes places overnight, so the bread is fresh every morning. Everyone who visits raves about how delicious our bread is! "EGGS BENI" CHICKEN COOP Notre Histoire

Our egg farm, home to 500 chickens, provides a good source of protein for our children. We are currently selling about 1,000 eggs a day to the local community with plans to increase production and sales.


FISH FARMS Notre Histoire

Among the largest fish farms in all of Haiti, our fish farms grow both tilapia, a high protein food source, and a variety of ornamental fish that are sold throughout the United States. Our home-grown tilapia is a staple in the diets of children and is sold throughout Haiti.


PWASON BENI RESTAURANT Notre Histoire

Our restaurant, Pwason Beini, prepares breakfast and lunch for Zanmi Beni staff and guests. The lunch menu showcases traditional Haitian preparations for tilapia, chicken, and beef. With the support of World Central Kitchen, we have renovated our kitchen with modern, professional cooking equipment, created a thirty-seat indoor dining room for the restaurant, and purchased a delivery van. Chef Samuel leads our five-person kitchen team to prepare and sell over 100 meals per day. With the new van, we are looking to expand our catering business and hopefully double our revenue in the coming year. As with our bakery, profits made through the business help to support the broader work of the orphanage and provide for the needs and care of our children.
