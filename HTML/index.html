Our Story Notre Histoire

Zanmi Beni was born out of terrible tragedy; the earthquake that devastated Haiti on January 12, 2010, and as a response to a great need; a group of extremely vulnerable children left homeless in the aftermath of the earthquake. Zanmi Beni est née d'une terrible tragédie. le séisme qui a dévasté Haïti le 12 janvier 2010, et en réponse à un grand besoin; un groupe d'enfants extrêmement vulnérables à gauche sans-abri à la suite du tremblement de terre.

These children, many of whom were disabled, orphaned, or abused, were living in a single ward at the General Hospital in Port-au-Prince, (Hôpital de l’Universite d’Etat d’Haiti, or HUEH). The children, many of whom had lived at the hospital ward for years, had been abandoned or had been left at the hospital by parents who were, sadly, unable to care for them. Pre-earthquake living conditions for the children were less than optimal, but when the quake hit, the building suffered severe damage and the already-overtaxed hospital staff, overwhelmed with throngs of severely injured people, were unable to even provide basic care for the children. When Paul Farmer, Loune Viaud and Nancy Dorsinville arrived at the hospital a few days after the earthquake, they found the children alone, hungry, frightened and surrounded by chaos, injury, disease, and death. It was apparent that something had to be done immediately to protect these children, and that it would be necessary to secure a safe and permanent home as soon as possible. Ces enfants, dont beaucoup étaient handicapés, orphelins ou maltraités, vivaient dans une seule salle à l'hôpital général à Port-au-Prince (Hôpital de l’Hôpital de l’Haïti ou HUEH). Les enfants, dont beaucoup avaient vécu à l'hôpital depuis des années, avaient été abandonnés ou avaient été laissés à l’hôpital par des parents qui étaient malheureusement dans l’incapacité de s’occuper d’eux. Il était évident qu'il fallait agir immédiatement pour protéger ces enfants et qu'il serait nécessaire de mettre en place un foyer sûr et permanent le plus rapidement possible.

Although its primary mission is to provide high-quality medical care to the Haitian poor, Partners In Health and its Haitian sister organization, Zanmi Lasante, have always taken care of children across its sites in the Central Plateau. They understood well the challenges they would face in accepting the responsibility of long-term accompaniment of this group of vulnerable children. However, they could not turn their back on the children’s’ plight and set out, in a very difficult post-earthquake environment, to receive them as family and develop a long-term, residential program where they would be safe, loved, and given the opportunity to grow and develop to their full potential. Bien que sa mission principale soit de fournir des soins médicaux de haute qualité aux pauvres haïtiens, Partners In Health et son organisation soeur haïtienne, Zanmi Lasante, a toujours pris soin des enfants sur ses sites du plateau central. It seemed almost miraculous when, in the midst of the devastation of the earthquake, Loune Viaud found a beautiful, sprawling property for sale just outside Port-au-Prince. Paul Farmer had recently celebrated his fiftieth birthday and generously donated the funds that were given to him as a birthday gift to help purchase the property. Partners In Health and Zanmi Lasante then formed a partnership with Operation Blessing, and together these three international organizations moved quickly and decisively to develop a plan to convert the property into an appropriate and high-quality living environment with the necessary accommodations for the children with disabilities. Existing buildings on the property were renovated, and new buildings were designed to meet the needs of the growing community. Staff was hired and trained, programs and daily schedules were created, and due to the yeoman’s efforts of a small group of dedicated people, the children were settled in their new home within four months. In the year following the earthquake, other orphaned, displaced, and abandoned children have joined the original group, bringing the total number of children in the Zanmi Beni community to sixty-four. Zanmi Beni, the name chosen for the community, means “Blessed Friends” in Haiti Creole. Indeed, all who are associated with the children of Zanmi Beni feel blessed to be a part of this beautiful and hopeful place. Paul Farmer avait récemment fêté son cinquantième anniversaire et avait généreusement fait don des fonds qui lui avaient été remis à titre de cadeau d'anniversaire pour aider à l'achat de la propriété. Our Mission Notre Mission

First, to offer a comprehensive and inclusive program where each child entrusted to our care is given the opportunity and support necessary to reach his or her full potential in a safe, stable, and loving environment. Premièrement, offrir un programme complet et inclusif où chaque enfant confié à nos soins se voit offrir la possibilité et l’appui nécessaires pour atteindre son plein potentiel dans un environnement sûr, stable et plein d’amour.

Second, to share our expertise and experience with the Haitian community and other organizations throughout Haiti that provide care for vulnerable and disabled children by actively supporting the development of high quality programs and services, providing technical assistance when needed, and offering support to the families of children with disabilities. Deuxièmement, partager notre expertise et notre expérience avec la communauté haïtienne et d’autres organisations dans tout le pays qui s'occupent des enfants vulnérables et handicapés en soutenant activement le développement de programmes et de services de haute qualité, en fournissant une assistance technique en cas de besoin et en offrant un soutien aux familles des enfants. OUR GUIDING PRINCIPLES NOS PRINCIPES DIRECTEURS

We believe caring for vulnerable children is our moral responsibility, and that all children are entitled to live with dignity and access to the goods and services that make human flourishing possible. We believe in an inclusive, family-based care model where in which each child is known and loved for who they uniquely are and each child has the opportunity to form long-term, loving relationships with adults and their peers. We strive to maintain a focus on gender equity and to create an atmosphere where tolerance, dignity, respect, and love prevail. We practice the “accompaniment” model by providing permanent access to food, shelter, medical services, education and companionship. We promote a positive view of disability in which each child is recognized as a person with rights and has access to full inclusion in all of life’s major activities. We are committed to developing on-site programs that will help to create sustainability by generating income and offer opportunities for self-sufficiency through job training and employment for the Zanmi Beni residents and staff. We believe in developing long-term partnerships with the local government, the private sector, and committed individuals to support the implementation of our mission. Nous pensons que prendre soin des enfants vulnérables est notre responsabilité morale et que tous les enfants ont le droit de vivre dans la dignité et d'avoir accès aux biens et services qui rendent l'épanouissement humain possible. Nous croyons en un modèle de prise en charge inclusif et familial dans lequel chaque enfant est connu et aimé pour qui il est unique et où chaque enfant a la possibilité de nouer des relations amoureuses à long terme avec les adultes et leurs pairs. Nous nous efforçons de maintenir l’équité entre les sexes et de créer un climat propice à la tolérance, à la dignité, au respect et à l’amour. Nous pratiquons le modèle de «l'accompagnement» en fournissant un accès permanent à la nourriture, au logement, aux services médicaux, à l'éducation et à la camaraderie. Nous promouvons une vision positive du handicap dans laquelle chaque enfant est reconnu comme une personne dotée de droits et ayant la possibilité de participer pleinement à toutes les activités principales de la vie. Nous nous sommes engagés à développer des programmes sur site qui contribueront à créer une durabilité en générant des revenus et en offrant des opportunités d’autosuffisance par le biais de formations professionnelles et d’emplois pour les résidents et le personnel de Zanmi Beni. Nous croyons au développement de partenariats à long terme avec le gouvernement local, le secteur privé et des personnes engagées pour soutenir la mise en œuvre de notre mission.

Our Partners Nos Partenaires

The founding, development, growth, and ongoing management of Zanmi Beni is a outstanding example of a collaborative effort among international organizations. We are grateful for the commitment and support of Partners In Health, Zanmi Lasante, and Operation Blessing: La création, le développement, la croissance et la gestion continue de Zanmi Beni est un exemple exceptionnel d’effort de collaboration entre organisations internationales. Nous sommes reconnaissants pour l’engagement et le soutien de Partners In Health, Zanmi Lasante et Operation Blessing:

Partners In Health (PIH) is a Boston based nonprofit organization whose mission is to provide a preferential option for the poor in health care. By establishing long-term relationships with sister organizations based in settings of poverty, Partners In Health strives to achieve two overarching goals: to bring the benefits of modern medical science to those most in need of them and to serve as an antidote to despair. Partners In Health (PIH) est une organisation à but non lucratif basée à Boston dont la mission est de fournir une option préférentielle aux personnes démunies dans le secteur de la santé. En établissant des relations à long terme avec des organisations sœurs basées dans des contextes de pauvreté, Partners In Health s'efforce d'atteindre deux objectifs fondamentaux: apporter les avantages de la science médicale moderne à ceux qui en ont le plus besoin et servir d'antidote au désespoir. www.pih.org

Zanmi Lasante is a sister organization to Partners In Health and is based in Haiti. It is PIH’s flagship project—the oldest, largest, most ambitious, and most replicated. PIH/ZL operates clinics and hospitals at 12 sites across Haiti’s Central Plateau and lower Artibonite. Today, Zanmi Lasante is the largest nongovernment health care provider in Haiti, serving an area of 1.3 million people and employing 5,400 Haitian citizens. Zanmi Lasante est une organisation sœur de Partners In Health et est basée à Haïti. C’est le projet phare de PIH - le plus ancien, le plus vaste, le plus ambitieux et le plus reproduit. PIH / ZL exploite des cliniques et des hôpitaux sur 12 sites sur le plateau central et le bas Artibonite. Aujourd'hui, Zanmi Lasante est le plus grand fournisseur de soins de santé non gouvernemental en Haïti, desservant une zone de 1,3 million de personnes et employant 5 400 citoyens haïtiens.

Operation Blessing International (OBI) is a nonprofit humanitarian organization dedicated to demonstrating God’s love by alleviating human need and suffering in the United States and around the world. One of the largest charities in America, OBI provides strategic relief in 23 countries on a daily basis through core programs such as disaster relief, medical aid, hunger relief, vulnerable children and orphan care, clean water and community development. Opération Blessing International (OBI) est une organisation humanitaire à but non lucratif vouée à démontrer l’amour de Dieu en soulageant les besoins et les souffrances des personnes aux États-Unis et dans le monde. OBI, l’un des plus grands organismes caritatifs d’Amérique, fournit quotidiennement des secours stratégiques dans 23 pays par le biais de programmes essentiels comme les secours en cas de catastrophe, les secours médicaux, la lutte contre la faim, les soins aux enfants vulnérables et aux orphelins, l’eau salubre et le développement communautaire. www.ob.org
